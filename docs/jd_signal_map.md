# JD Signal Map

## What the role really needs

The Senior AI Engineer role is mainly about production ranking, retrieval, candidate matching, embeddings, hybrid search, and evaluation. A good profile should show that the person has built or owned systems that help users find, rank, or match items, not just that they have used AI tools.

For this project, the strongest evidence comes from career history: search relevance, recommendation systems, ranking models, vector search, retrieval pipelines, and measurable product outcomes.

## Strong positive signals

Strong signals include target titles such as search engineer, ranking engineer, recommendation systems engineer, machine learning engineer, NLP engineer, and ML platform engineer.

The most important work evidence is production ranking/search/recommendation experience. Terms like learning to rank, search relevance, semantic search, candidate matching, reranking, vector search, FAISS, Milvus, BM25, and hybrid search should matter more when they appear in role descriptions and project ownership.

Evaluation is also important. The JD benefits from people who know how to measure retrieval and ranking quality with NDCG, MRR, MAP, precision@k, recall@k, relevance judgments, offline evaluation, online evaluation, and A/B tests.

## Medium signals

Product-company context is useful because ranking and discovery systems are often built inside marketplaces, SaaS products, recruiting tech, fintech, consumer internet, and e-commerce teams.

Location and education are supporting signals. Pune and Noida are preferred, while cities such as Hyderabad, Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Gurgaon, Bengaluru, and Chennai are still workable. Education in computer science, machine learning, AI, data science, IT, statistics, or mathematics can support the profile, but it should not outweigh career evidence.

Adjacent software, backend, data, platform, or analytics engineering backgrounds can become strong only when the career history includes real ML/search/retrieval work.

## Weak signals

LangChain, OpenAI API usage, ChatGPT, prompt engineering, RAG side projects, AI tools, and chatbot demos are weak by themselves. They can help later if they are attached to production retrieval or ML engineering work, but they should not push a wrong-role candidate above someone with real search or recommendation experience.

Computer vision, speech recognition, TTS, robotics, GANs, and image classification are valid AI areas, but they are less central to this JD unless the candidate also has NLP, retrieval, or ranking evidence.

## Negative/trap signals

Wrong-role AI keyword stuffing is a trap. A marketing manager, HR manager, operations manager, accountant, sales executive, content writer, business analyst, project manager, civil engineer, or mechanical engineer should not rank highly just because the profile lists AI keywords.

Service-company-only careers are not automatic rejects, but they need stronger product or system ownership evidence. Pure research without production deployment is also weaker for this role. Inactive profiles, irrelevant AI specialties, invalid platform data, and suspicious skill claims should later become penalties rather than hard rejections.
